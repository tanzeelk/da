India to organize world's largest virtual fair for textiles

Economic Times

The Indian government is planning a Textile India Fair featuring approximately 4,000–5,000 sellers and 25,000–30,000 buyers globally. Textiles Secretary Ravi Capoor announced this initiative at the GLOBIZ Global Textile & Home Furnishing Expo, organized by Ficci. Capoor emphasized that while India faces competition in the apparel sector, the home furnishings and textiles industry has significant growth potential, projecting exports could double within two years if properly developed.

Masks, surgical gowns & export boost the prices of organic cotton

Economic Times

Organic cotton prices have increased by 8 per cent to 12 per cent in the past two months and may remain firm as buying by the domestic medical textile sector and the overseas market has picked up. Prices have increased to Rs 39,000 candy of 356/kg each, and by over 15 per cent to Rs 210 per kg for export quality 30 carded yarn. There is also demand from Bangladesh and Vietnam.

How local communities in northeast are at the forefront of fight against coronavirus

Community Initiatives

Govindo Molsom, who works as a cook in Agartala, has not seen his family since March 23 when Tripura announced a lockdown. But the 36-year-old is not unduly worried as his family stays in a village called Kalagon Mausambari, some 50 km from the capital. The village is among the most protected places to be in this pandemic.
